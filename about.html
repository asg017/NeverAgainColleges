What this website is about.

About

Purpose

#NeverAgain Colleges serves two main purposes:
  1. Serve as real-time resource for universities that have publically supported participants in #NeverAgain movements.
  2. Encourage people to find and call on universities that have not put out statements yet.

High schools students want to demonstrate as part of the #NeverAgain movement, but they fear discipline from their school administrators. For some of them, they fear that any sort of disciplinary actions from their current schools will effect any current or potential admission to a university of their choice. By speaking out and putting out statements, university admission offices have the power to reassure their applicants and encourage their applicants to respectfully and peacefully perform their civic duty.

Methodology

A master spreadsheet is being maintained that serves as a real-time feed of universities that have put out public statements supporting current or potential prospective students who engage in peaceful demonstrations. There is a completed list of universities who have put out statements (along with a link to their blog/tweet), and a list of universities who have not (put in manually). Most of the data/statements has been provided by Chris Peterson of MIT, who has kept an extensive up-to-date list of schools/statements in the past few days (NACAC has one as well).
This website is a completely static website (hosted on Github Pages) that interacts with the master spreadsheet through an old Google Sheets API. Semantic UI us used to make it look pretty.

FAQ

Would applicants really get there admittance rescinded for disciplinary actions from their high school?

Yes - depending on the rule violation and the university in question. If an applicant broke a law or got expelled from school, some universities would rescind their applications as soon as they were notified. But, for some universities, there has been a grey area of what has been "acceptable". For example, are overdue library fees enough to be rescinded? What about too many detentions? Or too many abscences? By releasing a specific statement on "civic action" or supporting #NeverAgain marches and demonstrations, admission offices have a unique opportunity to concretely say what will be right or wrong for them.

If some schools have already spoken out for the #NeverAgain movement, why do we need more?

High school applicants apply to several universities. Some students may not feel comfortable participating in demonstrations if not 100% of the schools they apply to come out in support of the civic actions. By calling on all universities to make statements, we put their minds to rest.
