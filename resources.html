#NeverAgain Colleges Resources
Ways that college admission officers, counselors, or volunteers can help the #NeverAgain Movement.

About

What is #NeverAgainColleges Resources?

#NeverAgainColleges Resources will be a resource that high school students who were main coordinators in creating #NeverAgain demonstrations in their communities can use to aid them in the college admissions process.

For example, a student will be able to find people willing to edit their college admissions essays, or look over their Common App, or just general academic and career advice to help them succeed in the college admissions process.

Why is this needed?

#NeverAgainColleges Resources will provide a way young high school activists to easily navigate the complicated and intense world of college admissions. It also allows for people in the college admissions industry to give back to the #NeverAgain movement by providing their expertise to people that need it the most.

What type of help will be needed?

In general, I am thinking students would need help with:

  • College Essays - Ideas, editing, proofreading
  • College Choice - Finding colleges and universities that are a right fit
  • Academic and Carrer Advice - Giving tips, tricks, and information about different academic fields and career paths.

How would the selection process work?

This entire project is still a work in progress. However, I'm thinking it would be something like:

  1. A (verified) high school student who coordinated a #NeverAgain demonstration in their community would make a request from #NeverAgainColleges Resources.
  2. #NeverAgainColleges Resources would contact volunteers, and ask if they have the time/expertise to help the student.
  3. Once confirmed, the volunteer and the high school student would be put in contact, with clear rules on what type of help is/isn't allowed.

As a volunteer, how is my information protected?

The only people with access to your contact info will only be the #NeverAgainColleges team. That contact info is only shared with a high school student when you make clear and explicit consent to give it out, after the #NeverAgainColleges team shares with you the profile and needs of the high school student.
